# Across-session pupil position — results (N = 500)

Generated by `make_report.py` from `results.json`. Animal `AT-B1NO1`.

## Question

Days labeled "biased" (from the online gaze estimate) show gaze offset to one side; "centered" days do not. Test whether this reflects a real difference in pupil position or an artifact of the online pupil tracker.

## Data and method

- 14 sessions, 7 per condition. 500 frames per session, equally spaced across the full recording, decoded with ffmpeg. Frames with no detected pupil (blinks/closures) are dropped; detected counts are 319–494 per session (see table).
- Two pupil-center estimates per frame:
  - **robust ellipse** (`detect_pupil_ellipse`): dark-threshold → morphological open/close → largest contour with circularity ≥ 0.45 → ellipse center.
  - **online** (`get_pupil_online`): centroid of pixels in intensity band (0, 50] after a 3×3 erosion — the acquisition pipeline's method.
- **Eye-anchored frame** (`eye_frame`): per session, ≥5 landmarks clicked around the eye opening; PCA gives the horizontal axis `u` (along the fissure) and vertical axis `v`, normalized so ±1 = the landmark extent, 0 = center. The pupil center is projected into this frame. This is invariant to image crop, translation, and zoom; it is not invariant to out-of-plane head rotation relative to the camera (see Limitations).
- Per session, each axis is summarized by the **mean** over frames. The independent unit is the session; conditions are compared with a **Welch t-test on the 7 per-session means** (`_group_test`). A frame-level t-test is computed for reference only and is not used for inference (frames within a session are correlated → pseudo-replication).

## Result 1 — the two trackers agree (detection is faithful)

Pooled over all 14 sessions (per-frame, both trackers on identical frames):

- correlation: `x` r = 0.993, `y` r = 0.991
- median |online − robust|: `x` = 0.88 px, `y` = 0.67 px

Per-session correlations are 0.98–1.00. The online centroid tracks the same point as the shape-based ellipse; the largest per-session median |Δx| is 3.9 px (2026-03-19). See `figures/tracker_agreement.png`.

**A pupil-detection error is excluded**: both an independent shape-based detector and the online detector return the same pupil position.

## Result 2 — horizontal eye-frame position differs between conditions; vertical does not

Mean of the 7 per-session means, and Welch t-test on the per-session means (df ≈ 12):

| tracker | axis | centered | biased | t | p |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| robust | u (horizontal) | −0.069 | −0.109 | 3.018 | **0.0112** |
| robust | v (vertical) | −0.051 | −0.050 | −0.095 | 0.927 |
| online | u (horizontal) | −0.074 | −0.117 | 3.017 | **0.0111** |
| online | v (vertical) | −0.057 | −0.056 | −0.025 | 0.981 |

Biased days sit ~0.04 more negative in `u` (toward one corner), consistent across both trackers; the effect is horizontal only. Standardized effect size for `u` is large (Cohen's d ≈ 1.7). The two tracker rows are not independent tests (r = 0.99); effectively this is two axis tests, of which the horizontal is significant and the vertical null. See `figures/eyeframe_histograms.png`.

Note: all 14 sessions have negative `u` (−0.04 to −0.15); the between-condition difference (0.04) is about a third of this common offset.

## Result 3 — the tracker discrepancy does not differ between conditions

Per-session median (online − robust), compared between conditions (`compare_agreement`):

- Δx: centered mean −0.70 px, biased −1.40 px, Welch p = 0.356
- Δy: centered mean −0.25 px, biased −0.36 px, Welch p = 0.342

The online-vs-robust discrepancy is sub-pixel-to-few-pixels and statistically indistinguishable between conditions. See `figures/tracker_discrepancy.png`.

**A condition-dependent tracker bias is excluded**: the online method does not diverge from the robust method more on biased days.

## Result 4 — the signal is in the eye frame, not in raw image position

Per-session mean pupil `x`, compared two ways:

| raw image x / frame-width | 0.456 | 0.446 | −0.010 | 0.506 |
| eye-frame `u` (robust) | −0.069 | −0.109 | −0.040 | 0.0112 |

The between-condition difference is not present in the raw normalized image x (n.s.) and appears only after referencing the pupil to the eye landmarks. (The raw image measure is confounded by per-session crop and resolution and is reported only for contrast.)

## Interpretation

1. The day-to-day difference is **not a pupil-detection or online-tracker artifact**: an independent shape-based detector reproduces it (Result 1), and the online-vs-robust discrepancy does not vary with condition (Result 3).
2. The difference is a **real, horizontal shift of the pupil relative to the eye landmarks** (Result 2), significant at the session level and identical for both trackers. It is specifically horizontal; vertical position is unchanged.
3. This method cannot separate the two physical causes of that shift: (a) the animal fixating further to one side (eye rotation in the orbit), or (b) a small difference in head orientation relative to the fixed camera, which shifts the anterior pupil relative to the eyelid plane by parallax. Both produce the observed eye-frame shift. Distinguishing them requires a translation-invariant reference not tied to the eyelids (corneal reflection / pupil–CR vector, or a 3D eye model); the corneal reflections in these videos were too unstable to use.
4. For the on-screen gaze estimate this distinction does not matter **if calibration is per-session**: that day's geometry (including any head-pose component) is absorbed by the daily calibration, so the estimate reflects the real difference and is not an artifact. **If calibration is shared across days** and the cause is partly head reorientation, the on-screen estimate could be biased on some days; the eye-frame test alone does not rule that out. Calibration timing is the remaining open item.

## Limitations

- Session as unit: n = 7 per condition. The horizontal effect is significant (p = 0.011) with large effect size; the vertical null is not proof of no vertical effect at this n.
- The eye frame removes crop/translation/zoom but not out-of-plane head rotation (parallax); see Interpretation 3.
- `u`/`v` are normalized per session by that session's landmark extent, so they depend on landmark placement consistency; verify with `show_landmarks(date)`.
- Fixed threshold (online band 0–50, `high=50`); the day-to-day effect was previously checked to be stable across `high` in 40–60.
- Detection excludes closed-eye frames; 2026-03-02 has lower yield (319/500).
